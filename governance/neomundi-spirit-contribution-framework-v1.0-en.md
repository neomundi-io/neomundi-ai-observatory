# NeoMundi Spirit & Contribution Framework

**AI Observatory: Exploratory Cycle June - December 2026 - v1.0**

**Frozen version: 14 June 2026**

NeoMundi Recherche is opening a first contribution cycle around its AI Observatory.

The objective is simple: to better observe, better measure, and better understand the behaviours of AI systems.

This cycle is launched in a spirit of research, cooperation, and progressive construction. It deliberately begins in a light, sober, and pragmatic way: start, produce, learn, then structure further if the initiative grows.

## 1. The NeoMundi Spirit

We believe that every decision begins with observation.

We believe that every observation becomes more useful when it can be measured.

We always distinguish what is observed from what is interpreted.

We give priority to measurement over assertions, and to results over beliefs.

We know that power without measurement often produces an illusion of control.

We know that measurement without decision produces no change.

We seek a form of sovereignty grounded in understanding rather than blind trust.

We regard criticism as a tool for improvement, not as a threat.

We recognize each person's contributions, and we know that knowledge progresses more through cooperation than through appropriation.

We are not building an institution for its own sake.

The institution is not the end goal.

The goal is research, understanding, transmission, and the continuous improvement of our instruments.

We want structures that are strong enough to endure and flexible enough to evolve.

We reject both dissolution into chaos and the rigidity of dogma.

No status should shield anyone from criticism.

No authority should replace observation.

No belief should come before what can be measured, understood, and discussed.

Not the models.

Not the organizations.

Not the researchers.

Not the founders.

The instrument must remain in service of research, understanding, and the common good.

It must never become a pretext for ego, status, or personal power.

Because what matters is not who is speaking.

What matters is what can be observed, measured, understood, and transmitted.

## 2. Why Open a Contribution Cycle

The NeoMundi AI Observatory aims to produce regular measurements of AI system behaviours: stability, variability, drift, coherence, factual validity, cost, latency, and observation conditions.

To progress, this work needs different perspectives: researchers, engineers, data scientists, legal experts, domain experts, journalists, practitioners, advanced students, and competent citizens.

Expected contributions may be simple or more advanced: review, methodological critique, proposed questions, results analysis, visualization, writing, interpretation, dissemination, or protocol improvement.

## 3. Framework for the First Cycle

This first cycle is a six-month exploratory cycle.

It is based on voluntary contributions with a limited scope.

This current choice is due to the fact that the first cycle starts without a dedicated budget, with the objective of producing a first working foundation before a possible industrialization and funding phase.

It does not automatically create remuneration, a contract, employment, a mandate, a permanent role, governance rights, or any promise of future opportunity.

If the Observatory develops, the objective will be to move progressively through three stages:

1. start with a light and clean framework;
2. industrialize the methods, data, and publications;
3. institutionalize the initiative with a more structured legal, financial, and scientific framework.

At that later stage, budgets, contracts, assignments, or remuneration may be considered, but only through a separate and explicit agreement.

## 4. Contribution Principle

We want participation to be simple, loyal, and non-extractive.

This means:

- recognizing real contributions;
- not erasing the formalized work of contributors;
- not abusively capturing their inputs;
- not turning every general idea into a property claim;
- preserving the collective ability to move forward.

The central rule is simple:

> Ideas travel.
> Formalized contributions are attributed.
> Signed texts are respected.
> Sensitive data is protected.
> Published archives are stabilized.

A general idea, intuition, or discussion path does not automatically create a right to an exclusive claim.

A text, analysis, report, code, visualization, or formalized method must, however, be recognized according to the real contribution made.

## 5. Attribution

Any substantial contribution accepted and used by NeoMundi Recherche will be recognized in an appropriate manner.

This recognition may take several forms: mention, acknowledgement, citation, identified role, referenced contribution, or co-signature when the contribution justifies it.

Co-signature is not automatic. It depends on the real level of involvement, the nature of the contribution, and an explicit agreement before publication.

Contributor names are published only with their consent.

## 6. Confidentiality and Data Access

By default, contributors work on public, aggregated, anonymized, or pseudonymized information.

Access to sensitive data is not automatic.

Some information may remain restricted to the NeoMundi core team: the real identity of providers, internal mappings, keys, detailed costs, unpublished raw results, and technical or strategic data.

A separate confidentiality agreement may be required if a contribution requires access to non-public information.

## 7. Publication and Editorial Responsibility

NeoMundi Recherche retains editorial, methodological, and scientific responsibility for its publications.

NeoMundi Recherche may accept, refuse, defer, or request the revision of a contribution, notably for reasons of quality, method, confidentiality, coherence, security, legal risk, or alignment with the purpose of the Observatory.

Any substantial modification to a signed text is validated with its author.

A refusal of contribution is not a judgment on the person. It falls within the responsibility to maintain a reliable, readable, and sustainable framework.

Before publication, a contribution may be withdrawn or adjusted.

After publication, an archive may be corrected, contextualized, or supplemented. It is not intended to be arbitrarily deleted, except in the case of a serious issue: major error, confidentiality, legal risk, plagiarism, or consent obtained abusively.

## 8. Limits of Authority

Participation in the cycle does not confer membership in NeoMundi Recherche, nor the right to speak or act on its behalf, use its brand, or commit it, unless written authorization is provided.

Contributing to NeoMundi does not mean speaking for NeoMundi.

Conversely, NeoMundi Recherche does not claim authority over the work, frameworks, organizations, or institutions belonging to contributors.

Cooperation does not mean absorption.

## 9. Human Factor

Collective initiatives are not weakened only by technical problems.

They may also be weakened by the human factor: ego, fear of being erased, confusion of authority, implicit promises, rivalries between frameworks, unspoken commercial expectations, late-stage claims, or appropriation of general ideas.

This framework exists to reduce these frictions before they pollute the collective work.

We want to protect people, contributions, and the instrument.

But we also want to prevent ego, fear, or ambiguity from blocking research.

## 10. Translations

The French version of this framework is the reference version.

Courtesy translations may be provided to facilitate understanding and international participation, particularly in the native languages of contributors whenever possible.

These translations aim to respect the linguistic realities of contributing communities and to foster genuine, not merely decorative, openness.

Translations are not a decorative gesture. They affirm that AI governance must not be only technical or legal: it must also be cultural, linguistic, and genuinely accessible to the communities that contribute.

In the event of any divergence in interpretation, the French version shall prevail.

This framework is governed by French law.

## 11. Reference Glossary for Translations

Before any translation, certain terms must retain a stable meaning.

### Voluntary

An unpaid contribution within the current framework, freely consented to, creating neither employment, nor contract, nor any promise of future remuneration.

### Non-extractive participation

Participation in which formalized contributions are recognized, without turning general ideas into property claims or blocking claims.

### Formalized contribution

An identifiable and structured input: text, report, analysis, code, visualization, method, protocol, or substantial review.

### Attribution

Appropriate recognition of a real contribution: mention, acknowledgement, citation, identified role, referenced contribution, or co-signature when justified.

### Co-signature

Strong recognition reserved for substantial, formalized contributions that have been explicitly validated before publication.

### Editorial responsibility

NeoMundi Recherche's ability to accept, refuse, defer, correct, contextualize, or request the revision of a contribution in order to preserve the quality, coherence, security, and purpose of the Observatory.

### French reference version

The version that prevails in the event of divergence between multiple translations.

## 13. Simple Participation Agreement

To participate in the cycle, each contributor may confirm by email or form at the address or through the link indicated on the official NeoMundi Recherche contribution page.

The acceptance wording must always be linked to the French reference version. When a translation is provided, the acceptance wording may be presented in a bilingual version: the contributor's language of understanding + French.

Acceptance wording:

> “I have read and understood the NeoMundi Spirit & Contribution Framework - AI Observatory, 2026 exploratory cycle - v1.0, frozen version of 14 June 2026.

> I understand that my participation is voluntary within the current framework; that it creates no promise of remuneration or future opportunity; that my formalized contributions will be recognized according to their nature; and that general ideas discussed within the Observatory may circulate and be developed collectively.

> I authorize NeoMundi Recherche to reproduce, publish, archive, cite, and translate my formalized contributions within the framework of the Observatory, on a non-exclusive, non-commercial basis and with attribution - it being understood that I retain ownership of my reports and the freedom to reuse and valorize them.

> I guarantee that my contributions are original, that I hold the rights to them, and that they do not infringe the rights of third parties.

> I understand that participating in the cycle does not confer membership in NeoMundi Recherche, nor the right to speak or act on its behalf, use its brand, or commit it, unless written authorization is provided.

> I undertake to respect the rules of attribution, confidentiality, cooperation, and non-abusive claims set out in this framework.”
